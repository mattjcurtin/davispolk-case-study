The situation

A large-cap, Nasdaq-listed pharmaceutical company received an unsolicited public offer from a European strategic acquirer seeking a controlling stake, arriving simultaneously with a nomination notice for board seats.

Davis Polk was retained within 48 hours of the public announcement. The initial challenge was assessing the credibility of the acquirer's financing commitment, the regulatory approval timeline in both the US and EU, and the likelihood of the proxy contest succeeding given the existing activist ownership profile.

The Davis Polk approach

Regulatory strategy

Davis Polk coordinated with the SEC's Division of Corporation Finance to confirm the applicable tender offer timeline under Rule 14d-9 and assess whether the acquirer's simultaneous proxy solicitation violated the all-holders rule.

Transaction structuring / defense architecture

Davis Polk structured a shareholder rights plan with a 15% threshold calibrated to the activist ownership profile, paired with a market check process coordinated with three qualified strategic acquirers.

Multi-jurisdictional coordination

EU Merger Regulation Phase I review was projected at 25 working days — creating a structural timeline advantage. Davis Polk coordinated with European counsel to formally trigger the EU review process.

The outcome

The matter concluded 31 days from initial engagement with a negotiated transaction at a 22% premium to the original offer — achieved without any board seat concessions and without a shareholder vote.

  • EU Merger Regulation Phase I review completed without conditions
  • All three activist funds tendered their shares at the negotiated price
  • The 31-day resolution timeline has been cited in academic commentary on hostile defense strategy as a model for rights plan deployment combined with an accelerated market check
